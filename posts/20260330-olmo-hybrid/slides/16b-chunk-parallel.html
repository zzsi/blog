How Training Stays Fast: Chunk-Parallel

Yang et al., NeurIPS 2024

The problem

DeltaNet's recurrence $S_t = f(S_{t-1}, x_t)$ is sequential. Naively, training a length-$L$ sequence requires $L$ serial steps. GPUs need large parallel matmuls.

The solution: chunk it

Split the sequence into chunks of size $C$. Let $t$ = chunk index, $r$ = position within chunk ($1 \le r \le C$).

$S_t^{(r)} = \underbrace{S_t \cdot P_t^{(r)}}_{\text{carry from previous chunks}} + \underbrace{H_t^{(r)}}_{\text{new from this chunk}}$

$S_t$: state entering chunk $t$.
$P_t^{(r)}$: transition matrix after $r$ steps within the chunk.
$H_t^{(r)}$: contribution from the first $r$ tokens in the chunk.

Why this decomposition is exact

Each step is affine in the state: $S \leftarrow S \cdot A_i + B_i$, where $A_i = I - \beta_i k_i k_i^\top$ and $B_i = v_i k_i^\top$.

Unrolling from $S_t$:

$r{=}1:\; S_t \cdot A_1 + B_1$
$r{=}2:\; S_t \cdot A_1 A_2 + B_1 A_2 + B_2$

The $S_t$-dependent and independent parts separate cleanly:
$P_t^{(r)} = \prod_i A_i$, $\;H_t^{(r)} = \sum_i B_i \prod_{j>i} A_j$.

Result

Within chunk: $P$ and $H$ computed via dense matmuls (rank-1 structure of each $A_i$).

Across chunks: sequential depth drops from $L$ to $L/C$.
