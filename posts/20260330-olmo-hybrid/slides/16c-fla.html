flash-linear-attention (fla)

What is it?

An open-source library of efficient GPU kernels for linear attention and DeltaNet-family models. The key enabler for training GDN layers at scale.

Who maintains it?

Maintained by the lead authors of the DeltaNet training and GDN papers. Used by OLMo Hybrid, and referenced by multiple hybrid LLM teams.

GitHub: fla-org/flash-linear-attention

Supports: DeltaNet, Gated DeltaNet, GLA, linear attention, and more

Backend: Triton kernels (no custom CUDA needed)

Why it matters

Without fla, DeltaNet training would be sequential (one token at a time). fla implements chunk-parallel algorithms that make training throughput competitive with FlashAttention.

How it works (high level)

1. Chunk the sequence into blocks of size C (64-256)

2. Within each chunk: compute all outputs via dense matmuls (fully parallel)

3. Across chunks: propagate a single state update per boundary

Result: sequential depth drops from L to L/C

OLMo Hybrid slightly outperforms OLMo 3 in early training throughput benchmarks, despite the recurrent layers.
