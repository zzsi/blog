Hybrids Are More Than the Sum of Their Parts

Complementary strengths

Transformers

Can recall/copy from arbitrary positions.

Cannot maintain and update state sequentially (e.g., tracking variable assignments through a sequence: x=1, y=x+1, ...). This is a fundamental expressivity gap, not a training data issue.*

Linear RNNs (GDN)

Can track state (variable assignment, counting).

Cannot recall from arbitrary positions (bounded state).

Hybrid (GDN + Attention)

State tracking AND recall.

Can solve tasks neither can alone.

* Merrill & Sabharwal, 2023. Fixed-depth transformers are limited to TC0; state tracking requires NC1.

Key example: state-based recall

A task that requires:

1. Track state through a sequence (needs recurrence)

2. Retrieve a value based on current state (needs attention)

e.g., "which variable currently holds the largest value?" Requires tracking assignments AND looking up values.

Pure Transformer
Fails

Pure GDN
Fails

Hybrid
Solves it

Validated on synthetic tasks in the OLMo paper (Section 3.3). Hybrid models learn state-based recall perfectly. Neither pure architecture can.
