Why Negative Eigenvalues Matter: A Walkthrough

Sarrof et al. 2024, Grazzi et al. ICLR 2025 (Oral)

The eigenvalue along $k_t$

Assume $k_t$ is unit-normalized. $k_t k_t^\top$ is a rank-1 matrix with eigenvalue 1 along $k_t$ and 0 elsewhere.

So $I - 2\beta_t k_t k_t^\top$ has eigenvalues:

Along $k_t$: $\;1 - 2\beta_t$
All other directions: $\;1$ (unchanged)

When $\beta_t > 0.5$: eigenvalue along $k_t$ is negative. The state component in that direction flips sign.

Without the 2 (standard DeltaNet): eigenvalue is $1 - \beta_t \in (0,1)$. Can only shrink, never flip.

Proven result

Finite-precision LRNNs with only positive eigenvalues cannot solve parity for arbitrary-length sequences (Sarrof et al. 2024). Grazzi et al. extend this: GDN with negative eigenvalues can simulate any finite automaton.
