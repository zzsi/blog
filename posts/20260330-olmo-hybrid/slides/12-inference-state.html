GDN (30 heads), 485x smaller

Why is GDN so small?

Attention stores a KV cache: every past token's key and value vectors. Size grows linearly with sequence length.

GDN compresses the entire history into a fixed-size state matrix $S \in \mathbb{R}^{d_v \times d_k}$. New tokens update $S$ in place. No matter how long the context, the state is the same size.

OLMo Hybrid: 75% of layers use GDN (fixed state), 25% use attention (KV cache). The bottleneck shifts to those 25% of layers.

Result: serve longer contexts on the same GPU, or fit more concurrent users.
